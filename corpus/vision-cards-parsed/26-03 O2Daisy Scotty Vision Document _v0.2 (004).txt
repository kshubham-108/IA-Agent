Background / Problem & Opportunity / Scope Summary
1.1 Background
The Satellite market is early stage, but growing fast, with focus on LEOs to expand geographic coverage in rural/ remote areas. Two models are emerging for Direct-to-Device connectivity: MNO-led using operator spectrum and OEM-led using MSS satellite spectrum, with the latter posing a disintermediation risk to operators
VMO2 have signed an exclusive partnership with a Satellite operator to use O2 spectrum and that gives VMO2 first mover advantage and differentiation on satellites vs rest of market.
The Mobile Wholesale Services Agreement between VMO2 and O2 Daisy 
requires
 
both parties to discuss how the 
O2 Satellite service 
may be made available 
to O2 Daisy customers
. VMO2 have now made a commercial offer for the service. 
The intent of this Vision Document is to progress a
 shared 
understanding 
of how this service may be best integrated and 
made available O2 Daisy.

2 
Problem & Opportunity
 
Improved rural coverage, or improved coverage whilst on the move, is expected to be of interest to B2B 
customers;
 e.g. National Authorities and Mobile Workforce segments currently account for ~850k subscribers in B2B base.
1.
3 
 Scope
 Summary
 
The goal is to launch a new Bolt On into all B2B segments that seamlessly provides the O2 Satellite capability to those subscribers that have the Bolt On. When out of O2 network coverage, subscribers with the Bolt On will be able to ‘roam’ onto the Satellite coverage and continue to utilise SMS & Data.
It is expected that the Bolt On will be positioned to customers to provide coverage ‘peace of mind’ and/or as a ‘backup’ capability.
The Scope of the project should extend the current Consumer capability to provide 
provisioning and billing that can be integrated to the O2 Daisy OSS/BSS.
This is expected to include:
Service codes that can be provisioned via the wholesale provisioning interface
Offline CDR production to allow 
usage billing and reporting
Bon Voyage messaging
O2 Daisy will also need to 
create and launch the new Bolt On capability, and to make the necessary 
O2 Daisy OSS/BSS 
system and tariff changes to allow existing and new customers to take advantage of it.
O2 Daisy will use the same Optimised devices and applications as made available to the Consumer 
implementation, and
 is expected to benefit as this range evolves over time.
For initial launch, it is not expected that O2 Daisy will launch or certify any business-specific devices or applications
James Landale has created an initial solution architectural assessment, and this 
is includes
 in the Appendix.

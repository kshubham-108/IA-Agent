HLR to UDB Migration
Primary (SMART) Outcome statement
A significant number of customers remain on the legacy E/// HLR platforms. These customers were originally expected to be migrated through other projects that have since been discontinued. The E/// HLRs are now end of life, and while Core Engineering has secured an extension of vendor support until 2027, this deadline is fixed and cannot be extended further. In parallel, several major initiatives are underway within CIO to migrate customers from upstream legacy platforms, all of which must be completed by the end of 2026.
The remaining HLR customer base broadly falls into four categories:
Simple: No APN/MVR requirements and correctly provisioned SIMs.
Dependent: Customers with private APNs, static IPs, or requiring IMS uplift.
Complex: Legacy 2G services, bespoke APNs, and IoT use cases.
High Risk: Public sector customers with resilience requirements.
Successful migration will require close collaboration between CIO, CTO, and business owners to ensure data consistency and effective customer management alongside the technical transition.
All consumer customers, Tesco Mobile, giffgaff, and Smart Metering have already been migrated to the Nokia UDB. As a result, much of the required design and technical capability to support the remaining migrations is expected to be already in place.
 It should also be noted that there are already O2 Daisy/SMB customers that are on Nokia UDB today.

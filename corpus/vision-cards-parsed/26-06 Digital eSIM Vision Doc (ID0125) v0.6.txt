Background / Problem & Opportunity / Scope Summary
1.1 Background
eSIM technology has evolved since its initial introduction on the O2 network in 2019
Initial QR code voucher implementation sought to minimise changes from physical SIM processes; from warehouse stock management, customer delivery and to how Service agents supported customers, the changes were intentionally ‘light touch’
Newer eSIM standards allow for virtualised stock management, innovative customer delivery options, and enhanced Service support
.

2 
Problem & Opportunity
 
Channel feedback is that whilst ‘cost of change’ is key for customers, ease of ‘in life’ management is also a primary factor in selecting providers. Customers perceive eSIM capability as instrumental to addressing these.
The Sep’25 launch of an ‘eSIM only’ iPhone 17 Air raises a significant risk that the H2’26 launch of an iPhone 18 range might well adopt an ‘eSIM only’ approach across the iPhone range.
Support for the missing key Apple eSIM onboarding experience for new devices is considered essential to maximise O2 
Business
’ commercial outcomes from new device launches.

Out of Scope
The following are ‘out of scope’ of this Vision Document
changes to existing DISE eSIM capability (i.e. eSIM provisioning to LEPS via DISE should continue to be supported until these customers are migrated off DISE, and/or DISE is decommissioned)
All eSIM profiles will continue to utilise O2-managed subscriber credentials (K/OPc) within the O2 authentication domain. No separate authentication domain or AuC integration is in scope.

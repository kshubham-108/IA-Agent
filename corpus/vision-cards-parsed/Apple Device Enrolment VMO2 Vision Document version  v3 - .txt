Problem & Opportunity
 
State the main problem(s) and the opportunity created by addressing them. Highlight key pain points and expected benefits
Problem
Manual and duplicate processes are used to enrol 
and de-enrol 
Apple devices as separate per-customer orders are raised into the indirect portal for individual shipping. 
The existing manual Apple DEP enrolment process is not scalable, which prevents the transformation from migrating any significant number of SME and Soho customers to Daisy Central.
 
The 
existing Apple Device Enrolment process relies on DISE and Westcoast
‑
specific routing, which is not fully aligned with Daisy Central as the future system of record.
IMEI data will be available in Daisy Central, but the end
‑
to
‑
end automated feed into SRM is not yet fully established or approved within current CIO timescales.
O2 Daisy faces tight delivery timelines, and waiting for full assessment, build and approval risks delaying Apple device fulfilment for customers.
Any short
‑
term solution must continue to meet security, compliance and audit requirements, and must not introduce risk, rework or long
‑
term technical debt.
Opportunity
SRM (
Comarch
) has confirmed that where the file structure and outputs remain unchanged, existing Apple enrolment integrations can continue without redesign.
A phased approach enables:
An interim manual workaround to protect near
‑
term delivery, and
A strategic move to an automated Daisy Central → SRM feed as the steady
‑
state solution.
This allows O2 Daisy to maintain customer experience and Apple compliance without blocking on governance timelines, while still converging on the target architecture.
The approach is considered a small, low
‑
risk change for O2 Daisy, with SRM estimating 2
–
4 weeks of effort for testing once the automated feed is ready.

Appendices
Customers increasingly expect Apple devices to be delivered pre
‑
configured, secure and ready for use, particularly where Apple Device Enrolment (ADE) is required to apply device management and security policies prior to first use.
Today, Apple device enrolment is triggered once devices are procured, using IMEI data from the supply chain. Requests are processed through DISE, supported by Westcoast as an intermediary platform, before being passed to SRM, which converts validated enrolment files into API requests to Apple. This flow is proven but tightly coupled to legacy DISE
‑
centric integrations.
As part of O2 Daisy’s ongoing operating model simplification and the move towards Daisy Central as the primary orchestration platform, IMEI data will now be received from Westcoast directly into Daisy Central. However, the downstream Apple Device Enrolment process must continue to function without disruption, while aligning incrementally to the target architecture.
Given current CIO governance timelines (6–8 weeks) and de
‑
prioritisation of some supporting initiatives, a pragmatic, time
‑
bound interim approach is required to prevent delivery delays for O2 Daisy customers.
Out of Scope (for this Initiative)
Changes to Apple enrolment policies, customer configurations or Apple commercial arrangements.
Permanent reliance on manual processes beyond the agreed interim period.
Redesign or decommissioning of DISE outside this specific Apple Device Enrolment flow.
Any workaround that bypasses agreed security, compliance, or audit controls.

Digital MCAR Project
The high-level scope of the MCAR project involves the development and implementation of new data pipelines to fix business critical service messages and a new MCAR data model to drive marketing transformation. This new data model will capture and integrate both marketing attributes and customer events, providing a unified and comprehensive view of the customer. The project will address the challenges of fragmented data and manual processes, enabling VMO2 to deliver more targeted and efficient customer interactions, both service and marketing communications. 
Data Pipeline Efficiency
: Resolve instances of 'wrong comms' and significantly reduce the risk of repeated high-cost fines by implementing robust data pipelines.
Customer Engagement and Marketing Effectiveness
: Empower VMO2 with capabilities for improved customer engagement, increased Cross-Sell opportunities, a better Total Customer Experience (TCX), advanced personalisation, and a strong platform for future AI and agentic marketing initiatives.
Democratise Data
: Transition complex data logic into a transparent, managed operational model, fostering safer and more widespread data access and utilisation across the organisation.
Provide a Unified, Trusted Customer View
: Deliver a single, trusted view of the customer for Marketing by integrating the MCAR Attribute Model with Pipeline Event Streams, ensuring consistent insights across all channels.
Establish MCAR as the Martech / AdTech Centralised Intelligence Layer
: Position MCAR as the core intelligence data layer for current and future marketing and advertising technology, providing a definitive and consistent operational model for unified strategies, audience management and downstream customer platforms like Braze and

What is happening today?
Describe the current experience and known impacts (1–3 sentences)
Customer data is held across multiple upstream systems (
Netcracker
, ICOMS, CIO CDH, Data Modelling tables) and processed through separate, team-specific pipelines. 
The existing Customer Traits model processes ~2.7M customer rows daily via a batch pipeline that triggers at 8am, with results reaching Braze around 10am. 
Critical service comms like collections and network disruption run through independent pipelines — some with significant manual steps (e.g., offshore teams extracting files from ICOMS) and processing gaps (collections files only on weekdays; unplanned disruption comms paused over bank holidays).
What is the problem or opportunity
? 
Explain why the current state is undesirable, and what needs to change (1–3 sentences)
The current data landscape means VMO2 does not yet have a unified, real-time view of customer attributes for marketing and service activation. This has led to customer-impacting issues — for example, a June 2025 incident saw ~18,000 customers receive incorrect billing reminders because balance data had not been updated in time to reflect offline payments.
Key pipeline components lack built-in resilience for Ofcom-regulated service comms, data freshness relies on daily batch processing when near-real-time is needed, and high demand on the Data Democratisation and Data Engineering teams creates capacity constraints. 
MCAR is the opportunity to centralise and harmonise over 750 customer attributes into a single intelligence layer, enabling personalised 1-to-1 comms, real-time event-driven triggers, and future AI/agentic capabilities — while improving operational reliability and reducing team dependencies.

Is there anything explicitly out of scope?
Yes. MCAR is not an enterprise-wide Single Customer View (SCV); it is explicitly focused only on Marketing and Customer service data. Additionally, Digital Voice Migration (DVO/DVSOT) switch-off communications are explicitly ruled out of the general pipeline migration due to their own highly specific £23M regulatory risk profile. End of Contract and Best Tariff notifications (ECN/ABTN) for O2 business customers are also explicitly out of scope.

What is the approximate revenue uplift / cost saving from this?
The approximate financial benefits of the MCAR project are split between generating new revenue and saving costs through risk mitigation:
Revenue Uplift: The project is projected to generate up to £32.2 million in incremental consumer revenue by 2027. This is based on a targeted 0.5% to 1.5% performance uplift resulting from better personalization, improved cross-selling capabilities (such as targeting single-play customers for Volt convergence), and reduced customer churn.
Cost Savings (Risk Mitigation): The project is expected to save the business between £17.5 million and £66 by avoiding future regulatory fines. These savings will be achieved by automating critical service communication pipelines (like Price Rises and End of Contract Notifications) to eliminate the data inaccuracies and manual errors that currently result in "wrong comms" and regulatory penalties.

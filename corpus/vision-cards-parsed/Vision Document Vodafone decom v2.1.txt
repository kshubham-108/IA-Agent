Background or context of the opportunity
We are terminating Vodafone as a Wholesale Partner and therefore need to decommission their Wholesale Partner architecture.
Describe the problem or opportunity
This piece of demand is to decommission the Vodafone wholesale partner architecture and close down the related accounts and data holdings associated with the Vodafone Wholesale Agreement. The business needs a planning assessment to identify the essential Ofcom-regulated tasks required to decommission the Vodafone mobile MVNO from the O2 network and systems, together with an initial view of timelines, operating cost and any risks of leaving non-essential data in place. A similar decommissioning exercise was previously completed for Manx Telecom and can be used as a reference point.
Business Objectives - what is the business trying to achieve?
Objectiv
es 
Decommission the Vodafone wholesale architecture and close down accounts relating to the Vodafone Wholesale Agreement. Remove all IMSI/GT data. Complete a planning assessment covering the mandatory regulated activities, indicative timelines, associated OPEX costs and risks of retaining non-essential data in situ. The intention is to start in Q4.

future
 if we address this opportunity?
Vodafone will no longer be able to access VMO2 systems in the absence of a contractual trading agreement, and the legacy wholesale partner architecture and related accounts will be decommissioned as good operational practice.

For information: Next steps
 
Pre-submission checks: An early conversation with your Business Engagement and Mobilisation (BEM) Partner is advisable.  They may suggest a simpler delivery route. They may also be able to support early shaping of your vision around technical capabilities.
 
Submission: Once you have a potential sponsor, budget reference and priority from the person or body that prioritises demand in your division, please submit your draft Vision Card to the BEM team [Insert BEM team inbox here] 
 
Vision card review: A Business Engagement and Mobilisation Partner will engage with you to refine and clarify important details in your vision required to obtain the best early impact assessment. The BEM partner may drive conversations with key architects to ensure we obtain the most cost-effective recommendation ahead of sizing.
 
T-Shirt Sizing: When judged fit for purpose, the Vision card will be submitted for platform assessment. This will lead to an initial T-shirt-sizing, and clear ownership of delivery between TCS and TechM. 
 
Mobilisation: Further mobilisation tasks will depend on funding status, whether the idea is in the baseline plan, and future changes to the delivery quotation process that are TBC with the new delivery suppliers.

What customer need or problem is this proposal addressing
?
Problem:
Line managers are accountable for offboarding but operate across disconnected systems with no clear, consolidated view of responsibilities, resulting in inefficient execution, rework, and compliance risk.
Need:
A single, guided interface that orchestrates tasks, provides clarity, and ensures all required actions are completed correctly and on time.
 
What is happening today?
Describe what the primary customer (or colleague) experiences today, without interpreting or proposing change.  What is hard, confusing, slow, risky, or frustrating today?
Today
, line managers manage offboarding across multiple disconnected systems with no single view of their responsibilities, required actions, or progress. Tasks are coordinated manually through emails and separate systems, guidance is not centralised, and managers must actively chase updates, interpret requirements, and resolve issues, resulting in a time-consuming and uncertain experience.
H
ow do we know it matters to the customer?
We know this matters through direct evidence captured in user stories, process documentation, and current-state analysis. Line managers explicitly state the need for clearer visibility of responsibilities and tasks, indicating a lack of clarity in the current experience.
Current processes require managers to navigate multiple systems to understand status and next steps, and rely on manual coordination through emails and spreadsheets, demonstrating inefficiency and fragmentation.
Additionally, the need for exception handling, audit tracking, and 
compliance controls highlights existing operational risks in the current process.
Together, this evidence shows that managers are spending time interpreting and coordinating the process rather than executing it, confirming a genuine and material customer nee
What is the problem or opportunity? 
Explain why this current experience fails the customer or creates risk, confusion, or unmet expectations (1–3 sentences)
Line managers are accountable for offboarding but must navigate multiple disconnected systems with no clear, consolidated view of their responsibilities or progress, leading to manual coordination, delays, and risk of missed or incorrect actions.
This creates inefficiency, lack of confidence, and increased compliance and operational risk, as critical tasks depend on individual effort rather than a guided, controlled process.

Problem & Opportunity
 
VMO2 does not currently have a 
technical or 
commercially available 5G network slicing product that can be consumed by Wholesale Mobile partners. This creates a capability gap at a time when network slicing is becoming an increasingly important market opportunity, particularly for organisations that need differentiated mobile connectivity for priority or operationally critical use cases.
The opportunity is to enable O2 Business and agreed Wholesale Partners to take an initial slicing proposition to market using a controlled Phase 1 product. The current demand is strongest from emergency services and public sector opportunities, with a known pipeline including police force use cases. The Metropolitan Police has been identified as the primary current opportunity.
Competitor activity indicates that this is a developing commercial and strategic area. If VMO2 is unable to define, size and mobilise an initial slicing capability, there is a risk that O2 Business and Wholesale Partners may be constrained in responding to customer demand or competing for emerging priority connectivity opportunities.
Phased delivery approach
Phase 1 should focus on the initial launch capability: a static, SIM-based, always-on national slice that can be sold, ordered, provisioned, billed, reported and supported through agreed operational routes.
Phase 2 and later phases may consider more advanced capabilities such as dynamic slicing, private DNN, app-based routing, self-service portals or other product evolution features. These should be treated as future scope unless specifically confirmed for Phase 1.
The recommendation is to proceed with a phased IA approach, starting with Phase 1, so that VMO2 can confirm the cost, timeline, delivery route and scalability of the initial launch proposition before committing to broader product evolution.
Business 
ask
The business ask is to enable O2 Business and agreed Wholesale Partners to commercially launch a minimum viable 5G network slicing product. The product must be supportable end to end, including sales/order capture, eligibility, provisioning, lifecycle management, billing, reporting, partner onboarding/offboarding and operational support.
Create and manage the network slicing product with clear eligibility rules.
Support O2 Business and agreed partner order submission through an assisted sales journey.
Enable quoting and order journeys for eligible customers and subscriptions.
Provision an eligible SIM or customer onto the Phase 1 slice.
Identify slice-related billing events and usage over an agreed period to support charging and reconciliation.
Provide reporting on slice users, usage, performance and relevant service indicators.
Support partner onboarding and offboarding where Wholesale Partners are in scope.
Support customer onboarding and offboarding, including the process to remove the slice and return the customer to the public network where required.
Provide operational support for provisioning failures, incidents, billing queries and reporting discrepancies.
The focus for this IA is the Phase 1 initial launch. The IA should provide a clear cost, timeline and delivery-route assessment for O2 Business, and should also identify whether the capability can be reused or scaled for SMB Wholesale Partners. Where there are incremental

Phase 1 business outcomes
Enable a minimum viable 5G network slicing product for commercial launch
O2 Business and agreed Wholesale Partners can take a Phase 1 static, national, SIM-based slicing product to market for defined priority customer segments, starting with emergency services / public sector opportunities.
Enable partners to sell and order the slicing product through an agreed assisted route
O2 Business and agreed Wholesale Partners can capture and submit slicing orders using the agreed Phase 1 sales and ordering process, with clear eligibility rules for customer, SIM, device and network readiness.
Enable eligible customers to be provisioned onto a static national slice
Eligible customers can be activated onto an always-on national slice using 5GSA-enabled R15 SIMs and supported network capability, with onboarding and offboarding processes defined.
Enable lifecycle control of the slicing service
VMO2 and/or the relevant partner can add, remove, cease or change a customer’s slice entitlement, including the ability to return the customer to the public network where required.
Enable commercial charging and billing for slice usage
Slice usage and customer billing events can be identified and used to support the agreed charging model, including partner-level wholesale billing where Wholesale Partners are in scope.
Enable reporting to support commercial, operational and service management
Reporting can show the quantity of slice users, usage by customer or partner, relevant device information, performance information and any congestion/service indicators required for Phase 1 launch.
Establish a reusable capability for O2 Business and scalable Wholesale Partner rollout
The IA confirms whether a common reusable slicing capability can support O2 Business and future Wholesale Partner rollout, including any incremental cost, timeline or integration impact by partner or route.
Confirm the preferred IT delivery route for Phase 1
The IA provides a cost, timeline, complexity 
and
 impact view for the viable provisioning route options, including the tactical ABS route, direct 
Netcracker
 360 route, or any dual-running approach.
Protect existing network and customer experience
Phase 1 launch does not create unmanaged adverse impact to non-slice customers, 4G customers, public network users or existing priority voice/emergency service handling.
Create a controlled foundation for future slicing evolution
Phase 1 establishes a controlled MVP that can be expanded in later phases to include more advanced capabilities such as dynamic slicing, hybrid options, app-based routing, private DNN or self-service capability, subject to separate assessment and approval.
